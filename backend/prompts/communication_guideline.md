Carefully read through the entire transcript and follow these steps to create the communication guideline:
1. Analyze the overall communication style:
- Identify the general tone (e.g., formal, casual, professional, friendly)
- Note any recurring phrases or expressions
- Observe the complexity of language used (e.g., simple, technical, academic)
2. Examine the sentence structure:
- Determine if the speaker tends to use short, concise sentences or longer, more complex ones
- Note any patterns in sentence construction (e.g., use of questions, rhetorical devices)
3. Assess vocabulary usage:
- Identify any specialized terminology or jargon
- Note the level of vocabulary (e.g., simple, advanced, industry-specific)
4. Evaluate the speaker's approach to explaining concepts:
- Determine if they use analogies, examples, or storytelling
- Note how they structure their explanations (e.g., step-by-step, big picture to details)
5. Observe personal touches:
- Identify any personal anecdotes or experiences shared
- Note how the speaker relates to their audience
6. Analyze the speaker's persuasive techniques:
- Identify any rhetorical devices used (e.g., repetition, emotional appeals)
- Note how the speaker builds credibility or authority
7. Examine pacing and rhythm:
- Determine if the speaker uses pauses for emphasis
- Note any variations in speaking speed or energy
8. Identify any unique characteristics:
- Note any distinctive speech patterns or idiosyncrasies
- Observe any cultural or regional influences on language use
